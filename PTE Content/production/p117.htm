Occurrence and Production of Tennessine
Synthesis
Tennessine (then known as element 117 or ununseptium) was synthsised by bombarding berkelium-249 atoms with calcium atoms.
The experiment to produce element 117 began on July 28, 2009. In January 2010, scientists at the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions (in Russia) announced internally that they had detected the decay of a new element with atomic number 117 via two decay chains: one of an odd-odd isotope undergoing 6 alpha decays before spontaneous fission, and one of an odd-even isotope undergoing 3 alpha decays before fission. The obtained data from the experiment was sent to the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (in Livermore, California) for further analysis. On April 9, 2010, an official report was released in the journal Physical Review Letters identifying the isotopes as 294Ts and 293Ts, which were shown to have half-lives on the order of tens or hundreds of milliseconds.
