Occurrence and Production of Rutherfordium
Synthesis
Rutherfordium is created by bombarding plutonium-242 with neon-22 nuclei.
Although a half-life was not accurately determined, later calculations indicated that the product was most likely rutherfordium-259.
In 1969, researchers at the University of California, Berkeley conclusively synthesized the element by bombarding a californium-249 target with carbon-12 ions and measured the alpha decay of 257Rf, correlated with the daughter decay of nobelium-253.
