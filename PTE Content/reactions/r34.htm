Reactions of Selenium
Reactions with air
Selenium burns in air to form the selenium(IV) oxide.
  Se8(s) + 8O2(g)8SeO2(s)
Reactions with halogens
Selenium reacts with fluorine and burns to form the selenium(VI) fluoride.
  Se8(s) + 24F2(g)8SeF6(l)
Selenium (preferably suspended in CS2) reacts with chlorine and bromine to form diselenium dichloride and diselenium dibromide, respectively.
  Se8(s) + 4Cl2(g)4Se2Cl2(l)
  Se8(s) + 4Br2(l)4Se2Br2(l)
When carefully done, selenium reacts with fluorine at 0°C to form the selenium(IV) fluoride. Selenium also reacts under controlled conditions with the other halogens chlorine, bromine, and iodine, to form selenium(IV) tetrahalides.
  Se8(s) + 16F2(g)8SeF4(s)
  Se8(s) + 16Cl2(g)8SeCl4(s)
  Se8(s) + 16Br2(g)8SeBr4(s)
  Se8(s) + 16I2(g)8SeI4(s)
Reactions with acids
Selenium does not react with dilute non-oxidizing acids.
