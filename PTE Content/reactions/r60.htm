Reactions of Neodymium
Reactions with water
Neodymium is quite electropositive and reacts slowly with cold water and quite quickly with hot water to form neodymium hydroxide and hydrogen gas.
  2Nd(s) + 6H2O(g)2Nd(OH)3(aq) + 3H2(g)
Reactions with air
Neodymium tarnishes slowly in air and burns readily to form neodymium (III) oxide.
  4Nd + 3O22Nd2O3
Reactions with halogens
Neodymium reacts with all the halogens to form neodymium(III) halides.
  2Nd(s) + 3F2(g)2NdF3(s)
  2Nd(s) + 3Cl2(g)2NdCl3(s)
  2Nd(s) + 3Br2(g)2NdBr3(s)
  2Nd(s) + 3I2(g)2NdI3(s)
Reactions with acids
Neodymium dissolves readily in dilute sulphuric acid to form solutions containing the lilac aquated Nd(III) ion together with hydrogen gas.
  2Nd(s) + 3H2SO4(aq)2Nd3+(aq) + 3SO42-(aq) + 3H2(g)
