Chile
Lithium
In 2015, Chile produced 56 375 tonnes of lithium minerals. Lithium content from total world production of lithium minerals in 2015 was 26 200 tonnes.
In 2009, Chile produced 30 538 tonnes of lithium minerals (3rd behind Australia and China).
In 2006, Chile was the leading lithium metal producer in the world, where it is recovered from brine pools.
Sulfur
In 2006, Chile produced 1 million tonnes of sulfur. Total world production was 65.7 million tonnes.
Calcium
In 2015, Chile's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) was 860 075 tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2015 was 268.2 million tonnes.
Chile's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) in 2006 was 845 331 tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2006 was 142.5 millions tonnes.
Manganese
In 2009, Chile produced 1642 tonnes of manganese ore. World production of manganese ore in 2009 was 33.4m tonnes.
Iron
In 2015, Chile's production of iron ore was 15 449 000 tonnes. Total world production of iron ore in 2015 was 3.328 billion tonnes.
Chile's production of iron ore in 2006 was 8.629 million tonnes. Total world production of iron ore in 2006 was 1.81 billion tonnes.
Production of pig iron in Chile in 2006 was 1.121 million tonnes. Total world production of pig iron in 2006 was 925.8 million tonnes.
Copper
In 2015, Chile Republic's mine production of copper was 5.764m tonnes (making it the world's largest producer in 2015). Total world production of mined copper in 2015 was 19.2m tonnes.
In 2009, Chile's mine production of copper was 5.389m tonnes. Total world production of mined copper in 2009 was 15.8m tonnes.
In 2006, Chile's production of copper by mining was 5.36 million tonnes, total world production was 15.1 million tonnes.
In 2006, Chile produced 2.811 million tonnes of refined copper, total production of refined copper was 17.2 million tonnes.
Zinc
In 2015, Chile produced 48 071 tonnes of zinc from mining. World mine production of zinc in 2015 was 13 200 000 tonnes.
In 2009, Chile produced 27 801 tonnes of zinc. World production in 2009 was 11.4m tonnes.
Arsenic
In 2006, Chile produced approx. 10000 tonnes of white arsenic (As2O3).
Selenium
In 2015, Chile produced approx. 50 tonnes of selenium metal.
Molybdenum
In 2015, Chile mined 52 398 tonnes of molybdenum. World mine production of molybdenum in 2015 was 292 000 tonnes.
In 2009, Chile mined 34786 tonnes of molybdenum (third behind China and the USA). World mine production in 2009 was 231000 tonnes.
Chile is one of the world's largest producers of molybdenum. The Chuquicamata mine in northern Chile produces molybdenum as a byproduct of copper mining.
Silver
In 2015, Chile produced 1 504 271 kg of silver. World production of silver in 2015 was 27 511 000 kg.
In 2009, Chile produced 1301.018 tonnes of silver. World production of silver in 2009 was 22236 tonnes.
In 2007, Chile was one of the world's top producers of silver.
Iodine
In 2015, Chile produced 21 179 tonnes of iodine. World production of iodine in 2015 was 34 900 tonnes.
In 2009, Chile produced 17 399 tonnes of iodine. World production of iodine in 2009 was 29 200 tonnes.
In 2006, Chile produced 16 494 tonnes of iodine, 61% of the world total. Iodine is found in the mineral caliche, found in Chile, between the Andes and the sea.
Barium
In 2006, Chile produced 375 tonnes of barytes (BaSO4), the main source of barium. World production of barytes in 2006 was 8.8 million tonnes.
Rare Earths
It is estimated that in 2006, Chile produced 119000 tons of rare earth oxides.
Rhenium
In 2015, Armenia produced approx. 26 tonnes of rhenium, making it the world's largest producer.
Chile has the world's largest reserves, part of the copper ore deposits, and was the leading producer as of 2005.
