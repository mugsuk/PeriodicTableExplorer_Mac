Democratic Republic of Congo
Carbon (Diamond)
In 2015, Angola produced 14 284 000 carats of diamonds. Total production of diamonds in 2015 was 126.6m carats.
In 2009, the DRC produced 21.298m carats of diamonds (second behind Russia). Total production of diamonds in 2009 was 121.3m carats.
The DRC contains commercially viable diamond deposits. It is estimated that DRC has reserves of around 350 million carats.
Manganese
The DRC has important manganese deposits located in the Rift Valley.
Cobalt
There is frequently a considerable disparity between the cobalt content of ore raised, and cobalt actually recovered.
In 2015, the DRC produced 83 529 tonnes of cobalt metal. Total world production of cobalt metal in 2015 was 148 000 tonnes.
In 2009, the DRC produced 2950 tonnes of cobalt metal. Total world production of cobalt metal, in 2009, was 59300 tonnes.
In 2006, the DRC's mine production of cobalt was approx. 22000 tonnes. Total world production of cobalt metal, in 2006, was 53300 tonnes.

Radium
Deposits of the uranium-bearing ore that contains radium, pitchblende, are found in the DRC. After the discovery of tin in what is now Bisie, North Kivu in the Democratic Republic of Congo in 2002, illegal production has increased there to around 13500 tonnes. This is largely fueling the ongoing and recent conflicts there, as well as affecting international markets.
Protactinium
Protactinium occurs in pitchblende to the extent of about 1 part 231Pa per 10 million parts of ore (0.1 ppm). Some ores from the Democratic Republic of the Congo have about 3 ppm.
