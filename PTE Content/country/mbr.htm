Brazil
Lithium
In 2015, Brazil produced approx. 8500 tonnes of spodumene. Lithium content from total world production of lithium minerals in 2015 was 26 200 tonnes.
In 2009, Brazil produced approx. 15 000 tonnes of spodumene.
Brazil has deposits of spodumene (the most important of the lithium ores) LiAlSi2O6.
Beryllium
In 2006, Brazil produced 4 tonnes of beryl (Al2Be3Si6O18), one of the most important commercial compounds of beryllium. World production of beryl in 2006 was around 500 tonnes.
Carbon (Diamond)
In 2015, Brazil produced 31 826 carats of diamonds. Total production of diamonds in 2015 was 126.6m carats.
In 2009, Brazil produced 21153 carats of diamonds. Total production of diamonds in 2009 was 121.3m carats.
The first non-Indian diamonds were found in Brazil in 1725. In 2003 Brazil produced 0.4 million carats of diamonds.
Carbon (graphite)
In 2015, Brazil produced 75 100 tonnes of graphite. World production in 2015 was 2 200 000 tonnes.
Brazil has economically important sources of graphite. The United States Geological Survey states that in 2005 it produced 75600 tonnes and in 2006 it produced 75515 tonnes. Total world production of natural graphite in 2005 was 1.04 million tonnes and in 2006 was 1.03 million tonnes.
Magnesium
In 2015, Brazil produced approx. 5000 tonnes of magnesium metal. Total world production of magnesium metal was over 900 000 tonnes.
Aluminium
2015 production of aluminium was 772 200 tonnes. World production of aluminium in 2015 was 57.7m tonnes.
Brazil has large deposits of bauxite, aluminium ore, and is one of the primary mining countries. 2009 production of aluminium was 1.535m tonnes. World production of aluminium in 2009 was 36.9m tonnes.
