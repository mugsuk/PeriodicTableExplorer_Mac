Algeria
Helium
In the mid-1990s, a new plant in Arzew, Algeria producing 1.7 x 107 m3 began operation, with enough production to cover all of Europe's demand. In 2004-2006 a plant in Skikda was built, and production launched in April 2007, two years after the proposed launch.
Production in 2007 stood at 240 000 000 cubic feet (approx. 6.796 million cubic metres).
Algeria quickly became the second leading producer of helium. Figures for 2002 show the USA produced 86% of the world's helium, Algeria 9% and Russia/Poland 5%.
Calcium
In 2015, Algeria's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) was approx. 2 000 000 tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2015 was 268.2 million tonnes.
Algeria's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) in 2006 was 1.033 million tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2006 was 142.5 millions tonnes.
Iron
In 2015, Algeria's production of iron ore was 576 000 tonnes. Total world production of iron ore in 2015 was 3.328 billion tonnes.
Algeria's production of iron ore in 2006 was 2.34 million tonnes tonnes. Total world production of iron ore in 2006 was 1.81 billion tonnes.
Production of pig iron in Algeria in 2006 was 1.093 million tonnes. Total world production of pig iron in 2006 was 925.8 million tonnes.
Silver
In 2014, Algeria produced 16 kg of silver. World production of silver in 2014 was 27 203 000 kg.
In 2009, Algeria produced 200 kg of silver. World production of silver in 2009 was 22236 tonnes.
Barium
In 2015, Algeria produced approx. 30 000 tonnes of barytes (BaSO4), the main source of barium. World production of barytes in 2015 was 7.9 million tonnes.
In 2006, Algeria produced 64787 tonnes of barytes (BaSO4), the main source of barium. World production of barytes in 2006 was 8.8 million tonnes.
Gold
In 2015, Algeria's mine production of gold was approx. 80 kg. Total amount of gold mined in 2015 was 3110 tonnes.
In 2009, Algeria's mine production of gold was 998 kg. Total amount of gold mined in 2009 was 2460 tonnes.
In 2006, Algeria's mine production of gold was 377 kg. Total amount of gold mined in 2006 was 2310 tonnes.
Mercury
Algeria's mercury production has dropped from 3.07 tonnes in 2002, to 0.3 tonnes in 2005 to zero by 2006.
Lead
