Mercury
In 2002, Spain produced over 726 tonnes of mercury (33% of the world's total), the following year this rose to 745 tonnes (35%). 2004-2006 saw production drop to zero.
Beginning in 1558, with the invention of the patio process to extract silver from ore using mercury, mercury became an essential resource in the economy of Spain and its American colonies.
