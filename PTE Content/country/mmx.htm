Mexico
Carbon (graphite)
In 2015, Mexico produced 8071 tonnes of graphite. World production in 2015 was 2 200 000 tonnes.
Mexico has economically important sources of graphite. The United States Geological Survey states that in 2005 it produced 12357 tonnes and in 2006 it produced 12500 tonnes. Total world production of natural graphite in 2005 was 1.04 million tonnes and in 2006 was 1.03 million tonnes.
Sulfur
In 2006, Mexico produced 1.774 million tonnes of sulfur. Total world production was 65.7 million tonnes.
Calcium
In 2015, Mexico's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) was approx. 7 100 000 tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2015 was 268.2 million tonnes.
In 2006, Mexico produced 936 433 tonnes of fluorspar (CaF2). World production was around 5.5 million tonnes.
Reserves of fluorspar in Mexico are thought to be 32 million tonnes.
Mexico's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) in 2006 was approx. 6 million tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2006 was 142.5 millions tonnes.
Vast crystals of gypsum up to 10 metres in length have been found in the "Cueva de los Cristales" in the Naica Mine in the Mexican state of Chihuahua.
