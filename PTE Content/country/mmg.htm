Madagascar
Beryllium
In 2006, Madagascar produced around 12 tonnes of beryl (Al2Be3Si6O18), one of the most important commercial compounds of beryllium. World production of beryl in 2006 was around 500 tonnes.
As of 1999, the largest known crystal of any mineral in the world is a crystal of beryl from Madagascar, 18 metres long and 3.5 metres in diameter.
Carbon (graphite)
In 2015, Madagascar produced 8000 tonnes of graphite. World production in 2015 was 2 200 000 tonnes.
Madagascar produced 15000 tonnes in both 2005 and 2006 of "lump" or "vein" graphite.
Calcium
In 2015, Madagascar's production of gypsum (CaSO4.2H2O) was approx. 130 tonnes. World production of gypsum in 2015 was 268.2 million tonnes.
Scandium
Madagascar and the Iveland-Evje Region in Norway are the only minable deposits of minerals with high scandium content, thortveitite (Y,Sc)2(Si2O7) and kolbeckite (ScPO4.2H2O).
Titanium
In 2015, Madagascar produced approx. 200 000 tonnes of ilmenite (FeTiO3), and approx. 11 000 tonnes of rutile (TiO2), the only two commercially important titanium ores.
Chromium
In 2015, Madagascar produced 197 800 tonnes of chromium ores and concentrates. Total worldwide production in 2015 was 35.3 million tonnes.
In 2006, Madagascar produced 132 335 tonnes of chromium ores and concentrates. Total worldwide production in 2006 was 21.2 million tonnes.
Cobalt
There is frequently a considerable disparity between the cobalt content of ore raised, and cobalt actually recovered.
In 2015, Madagascar produced 3920 tonnes of cobalt metal. Total world production of cobalt metal in 2015 was 148 000 tonnes.
Nickel
In 2015, Madagascar mined 47 271 tonnes of nickel metal. World total nickel production by mining in 2015 was 2 092 000 tonnes.
Zirconium
In 2015, Madagascar produced approx. 25000 tonnes of zircon (ZrSiO4). The total amount of zircon produced in 2015 was 1 341 000 tonnes.
Gold
In 2015, Madagascar's mine production of gold was 3500 kg. Total amount of gold mined in 2015 was 3110 tonnes.
