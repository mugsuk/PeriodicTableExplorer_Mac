Bohr Model
Introduced in 1913 the Bohr model, devised by Niels Bohr, depicts the atom as a small, positively charged nucleus surrounded by electrons that travel in circular orbits around the nucleus - similar in structure to the solar system, but instead of gravity, electrostatic forces provide attraction.
Since the Bohr model is a quantum physics-based modification of the Rutherford model, many sources combine the two, referring instead to the Rutherford-Bohr model.
